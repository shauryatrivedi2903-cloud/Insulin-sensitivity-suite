Results & Interpretation
    • HOMA-IR = (Glucose × Insulin) / 405
      Typical: < 2.0 Normal • 2.0–2.5 Borderline • > 2.5 Insulin resistance
      Ref: Matthews DR et al. Diabetologia 1985;28:412–419.
    • HOMA-BETA = (20 × Insulin) / (Glucose − 3.5)
      Typical: 60–140% Normal β-cell function • < 60% Decreased • > 140% High/compensatory
      Ref: Matthews DR et al. 1985.
    • QUICKI = 1 / [log(Insulin) + log(Glucose)]
      Typical: > 0.33 Normal • 0.30–0.33 Borderline • < 0.30 Insulin resistance
      Ref: Katz A et al. J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2000;85:2402–2410.
    • Matsuda Index = 10000 / √(FPG × FPI × Mean G × Mean I)
      Typical: > 4 Normal • 2.5–4 Impaired • < 2.5 Resistant
      Ref: Matsuda M, DeFronzo RA. Diabetes Care 1999;22:1462–1470.
    • ISI (0,120) = 10000 / √(G₀ × I₀ × G₁₂₀ × I₁₂₀)
      Typical: > 4.3 Normal • 2.9–4.3 Impaired • < 2.9 Resistant
      Ref: Derived from OGTT sensitivity indices (Matsuda/DeFronzo framework).
    • Insulinogenic Index (IGI) = (Ins₃₀ − Ins₀) / (Gluc₃₀ − Gluc₀)
      Early insulin response; higher = better β-cell function (no universal cut-offs; cohort-dependent).
      Ref: Kosaka K et al. Diabetes 1970;19:188–192.
    • Disposition Index (DI) = IGI × Matsuda Index
      Composite β-cell compensation for insulin sensitivity; higher = better compensation.
      Ref: Kahn SE. Diabetologia 2003;46:3–19.
